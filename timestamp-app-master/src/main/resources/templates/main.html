What is a Timestamp?

A timestamp is a digital record marking the date and time a specific event occurred. Timestamps ensure transparency, integrity, and accountability, playing a crucial role in legal documentation, data security, project management, and more.

Why Are Timestamps Important?

Timestamps play a pivotal role in maintaining the authenticity and reliability of digital records. They help verify authenticity, track changes, maintain compliance, and enhance collaboration.
